Good long-term resilience because AI supports design and monitoring, but embedded systems, control, instrumentation, and real-world integration still need engineers. Could be a more integrated electronics-and-computing path than some local options, especially if he wants less separation between software and hardware.
Ingeniería Mecatrónica Medium Good if he is serious about robotics, automation, controls, and manufacturing systems. It is exciting and future-facing, but still depends on a heavier math/physics/engineering base. Private-university pricing via CETYS tuition simulator; cost should be treated as materially different from nearby CSU public pricing. Excellent AI-era relevance because robotics, automation, industrial networks, control, and physical systems are growing areas where AI is a tool, not a substitute. Could be stronger than a pure EE route if the real interest is robotics and industrial automation rather than classic electrical theory.
Ingeniería Industrial Medium Worth considering if he wants operations, process improvement, quality, supply chain, and technology-enabled optimization rather than deep hardware design. Private-university pricing via CETYS tuition simulator; scholarship availability may matter a lot here for actual affordability. Moderately resilient. AI automates some forecasting and reporting tasks, but operations leadership, systems improvement, quality, and supply-chain execution remain human-led. Potentially faster and more flexible if he wants strong employability in manufacturing-heavy regional markets without the full burden of EE.

India Options

Engineering colleges in India that are strong options for American students Planning View

This section focuses on schools with strong engineering reputation plus clear international-student pathways, English-medium instruction, or structured support for foreign students.

Most prestigious

IIT Delhi, IIT Bombay, and IIT Madras are the strongest engineering names in India in current global rankings.

Most practical for Americans

VIT and Manipal (MAHE) are easier to approach because they have formal international admissions workflows and clearer support for foreign students.

Important reality check

The IITs are elite, but undergraduate admission for foreign nationals is still highly selective. For many U.S. students, strong private options may be more realistic.
